\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

The questions that decide energy-transition policy---who pays, when the costs and
benefits arrive, what cleaner air is worth and to whom, how the budget closes---live
in the seam between two modelling traditions that have been joined many times, but
never with an economy that has ages in it. This paper introduced a channel-based
soft link between \CLEWS{} and \OGCore{} that fills that cell: bottom-up
energy-water-land technology on one side, an overlapping-generations economy with
fiscal closure on the other, connected by a small set of named, auditable
mechanisms. The Philippine coupling shows why the missing structure was the
load-bearing one: the reform's macroeconomic sign depends on the horizon, its health
co-benefits act through demography, and its incidence is regressive even under
revenue recycling---findings that are outputs of the coupled tool and inexpressible
in its parents or predecessors. The framework is deliberately unfinished at three
labelled seams---loop closure, unit calibration, energy as a priced input---and
deliberately finished where policy work needs it to be: every number in the coupled
run is attributable to a mechanism a reader can inspect, contest, and re-run.
