\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

The economic questions raised by the energy transition---who bears the costs and
benefits, when they arrive, what the health co-benefits are worth and to whom, and
how the public budget closes---fall between two modelling traditions that have been
integrated many times, but never with an economy that carries age structure. This
paper introduced a channel-based soft link between \CLEWS{} and \OGCore{} that
supplies the missing combination: bottom-up energy-water-land technology on one
side, an overlapping-generations economy with fiscal closure on the other, connected
by a small set of named, auditable mechanisms. The Philippine application shows why
the added structure is load-bearing: the reform's macroeconomic effect changes sign
with the horizon, its health co-benefits operate through demography, and its
incidence is regressive even under revenue recycling---results that are outputs of
the coupled framework and are not expressible in its parents or predecessors. The
framework is deliberately limited at three labelled interfaces---loop closure, unit
calibration, and energy as a priced input---and deliberately complete where policy
use requires it to be: every number in the coupled run is attributable to a
mechanism that a reader can inspect, contest, and re-run.
