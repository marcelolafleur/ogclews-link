\section{Illustration: the Philippine coupled run}
\label{sec:results}

We illustrate the framework end-to-end on the Philippine calibration (\OGCore{} via the
\texttt{ogphl} multi-sector build, $\Mind=8$ industries, $\Igood=5$ goods; electricity is
industry $m_e=2$), coupled to a \CLEWS{}/\OSeMOSYS{} scenario pair (a baseline and a
policy/decarbonisation reform). This demonstrates that the machinery solves, that the
signals cross the seam correctly, and that the signs and channels behave as
\Cref{sec:channels} predicts---not a policy evaluation. All figures below are the
committed M=8 golden record (\texttt{results/golden.json}); rates and wages are reported,
like the quantities, as percent changes from baseline.

\subsection{The flagship coupled run}
\label{sec:coupled}
The \chan{coupled} experiment applies, in one reform: the real \CLEWS{} electricity price
(the ``auto'' source---a cost-of-electricity index or the reconstructed levelized cost,
\Cref{sec:signal}) through the composite transmission of \Cref{sec:routes}
(inter-industry cost-push plus a recycled household wedge); public grid investment; a
\$50/tCO\textsubscript{2} carbon price on the \CLEWS{} side; and the Global Burden of
Disease health channel; then it emits the equilibrium return and activity back to
\CLEWS{}. The real Philippine electricity price rises about $5\%$ on average
(\Cref{sec:calibration}); the coupled run moves electricity output by $-2.0\%$.

The response is a positive transition and a negative steady state
(\Cref{tab:phl-coupled}): output is $+0.18\%$ near-term and $+0.20\%$ by period ten, as
the working-age morbidity gain raises effective labour, but $-0.14\%$ in the long-run
steady state, where the added retirees (the cleaner-air survival gain skews elderly)
combine with the contractionary price channels (\Cref{fig:transition}). This horizon
split---a positive path, a negative steady state---is the signature of the demographic
health channel and is invisible to a static core.

\subsection{Channel decomposition}
\label{sec:decomposition}
Table~\ref{tab:phl-channels} runs each channel as a standalone reform;
\Cref{fig:channel-impact} plots the steady-state output impacts. Four things stand out,
all as \Cref{sec:channels} anticipates.
The \emph{energy-price} channel is small ($Y_{ss}=-0.006\%$): the real Philippine
electricity price rises about $5\%$ and, honestly transmitted, moves aggregate output
little---the illustrative $+20\%$ shocks of earlier work were doing the work, not the
data. \emph{Carbon} is the clearest standalone contraction ($Y_{ss}=-0.021\%$): a
resource cost that shrinks capital and labour. \emph{Capital intensity} barely moves
aggregate output ($+0.001\%$) but reshapes the energy sector (\Cref{sec:ch-capint}).
\emph{Health} is near-zero at the steady state ($-0.0003\%$) but $+0.19\%$
near-term---the morbidity productivity gain.
The forward emitters (\chan{discount\_rate}, \chan{demand}) and \chan{investment} are
$\approx 0$ as standalone reforms: the Philippine public grid-capex delta is small, and
the emitters only feed \CLEWS{} until the loop closes. Finally, the coupled steady state
($-0.138\%$) is well beyond the sum of the standalone channels ($\approx-0.02\%$): the
channels interact in general equilibrium, so the standalone table is a diagnostic
reading, not an additive accounting of the coupled run.

\subsection{Distributional incidence}
\label{sec:incidence}
The distinctive output of an \OGCore{} core is incidence. Under the recycled
electricity-price change with energy a partial necessity (\chan{clean\_incidence}),
aggregate consumption falls ($C_{ss}=-0.092\%$) even as the price change is returned as
transfers: the burden lands more heavily on lower-income households, who spend a larger
share on energy---a regressive pattern a single-agent macro core cannot produce.

\subsection{The capital pair}
\label{sec:capital-results}
The \chan{capital\_intensity} channel is decomposed exactly via the firm identity of
\Cref{sec:ch-capint} (\Cref{tab:capital-pair}, measured on an earlier steady-state solve
of the lever): raising the energy capital exponent
$12.2\%$ collapses the electricity price $42.8\%$; demand is elastic enough that output
\emph{rises} $20.9\%$, yet capital \emph{falls} $22.4\%$ because the price collapse shrinks
revenue---and the identity $\hat K_E=\hat\gamma_E\hat p_E\hat Y_E$ reproduces that response
to within $0.03\%$. It is a factor-share and energy-price lever, not a capital-draw-in.

\subsection{Validation}
\label{sec:validation}
The coupling is exercised by a continuable, grouped \emph{battery}
(\texttt{experiments/run\_battery.py}) run the canonical cross-environment way---the link
orchestrates in its own environment and subprocesses the country's \OGCore{} model in the
\texttt{ogphl} environment via the model registry, exchanging data files (no shared
interpreter). All items pass the hard gate (the solve completes); macro aggregates are
regression-locked in the committed golden record (\texttt{results/golden.json}), checked
by \texttt{tests/test\_golden.py}, alongside a transform-level unit suite. One documented
exception: the lives-saved health reform leaves an intrinsic Walras residual that scales
with the target, so its steady-state resource-constraint gate is loosened to $10^{-5}$
(an order of magnitude under \OGCore{}'s own transition-path default), while the
deaths-added direction stays at the tight default.

\caveat{The energy-price and health inputs are real (a \CLEWS{} price ratio and the GBD
burden); the carbon and investment \emph{levels} still ride on the uncalibrated
unit/deflator bridge (\Cref{sec:calibration}), so treat those two magnitudes as
illustrative.}
