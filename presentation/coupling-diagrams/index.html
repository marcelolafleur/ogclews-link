OG-Core ⇄ CLEWS · coupling diagrams

How the macroeconomic model and the energy model couple

Five figures, one shared design language. Colour marks where a thing sits — teal is CLEWS, blue is OG-Core, red is policy — and line style marks how much to trust a result, whether to read its size or only its direction, so each figure reads the same in greyscale and to a colour-blind reader.

Visual and verbal language calibrated against Edward Tufte and the FT / Nature / Quanta house styles: a warm paper ground, a serif declarative title over a grey standfirst, restrained colour, and a source line. Each figure carries its own heading. Magnitudes are omitted until the 8-industry (M=8) run lands.

Rebuild a figure: cd presentation/coupling-diagrams && latexmk -pdf <name>.tex, then pdftoppm -png -r 150 <name>.pdf <name>. Shared palette + styles in _preamble.tex (colour from ../theme/ogclews-colors.tex); a Nature/journal cut flips to a white ground via \def\ogground{white}. On branch presentation-narrative.
