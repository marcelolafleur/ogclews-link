\section{The seven channels — how the two worlds connect}

\begin{frame}{Seven cause-and-effect links}
  Seven channels connect the energy system and the economy — each a simple cause-and-effect story:
  \smallskip
  {\small
  \begin{itemize}\setlength\itemsep{2.5pt}
    \item \textbf{Health} — cleaner air means healthier, more productive people
    \item \textbf{Energy prices} — system costs reach household bills, and hit poorer families hardest
    \item \textbf{Carbon price} — one price pushes the energy system cleaner \emph{and} raises
          revenue that can be returned to households
    \item \textbf{Investment} — building the public grid is a public cost, funded by debt or taxes
    \item \textbf{Capital intensity} — a clean power system is capital-heavy, so it pulls in investment
          and shifts the mix away from labour (the private twin of investment)
    \item \textbf{Cost of capital} — how patient the economy is shapes which projects get built
    \item \textbf{Demand} — a growing economy needs more energy
  \end{itemize}}
  \smallskip
  {\color{sub}\small The plain flows follow; the full variable-level map is in the appendix.}
\end{frame}

\section{Rigor \& roadmap}

\dgmframe{Where each channel sits — and where it is heading}{maturity}{%
  Filled = today; open = target. The practical frontier is a ladder of approximations to the dual-exchanging theoretical best.}

\begin{frame}{What works today}
  \begin{itemize}\setlength\itemsep{6pt}
    \item The energy system's results — prices, build costs, emissions — flow into the economy, which
          is then solved in full, \emph{including who is affected} across income groups.
    \item On a real test case it reproduced a roughly 17\% fall in energy use and a clear pattern of
          who bears the cost.
    \item The economy also produces the signals the energy system needs back: energy demand and the
          interest rate.
    \item Every building block is unit-tested.
  \end{itemize}
  \medskip
  {\color{sub}\small What isn't finished yet is shown per channel on the roadmap and listed under
   Next steps — chiefly the automatic back-and-forth loop and calibrating the sizes.}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Next steps}
  \begin{itemize}\setlength\itemsep{5pt}
    \item Feed in the energy system's real prices from a full model run.
    \item Fix the money units so the cost and revenue sizes can be trusted.
    \item Close the loop — re-solve the two models back and forth automatically until they agree.
    \item Refine how avoided illness becomes worker productivity (the pollution$\to$deaths link is now calibrated from real data).
    \item Plug this in to MUIOGO, the planning tool, as its economy engine.
  \end{itemize}
  \medskip
  \begin{block}{One line}
    A small, careful library of cause-and-effect links — a step beyond the classic energy-economy
    models, using a richer economy that tracks households, government, and who is affected.
  \end{block}
\end{frame}
