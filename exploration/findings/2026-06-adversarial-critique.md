## Remittances

**Overall assessments (per lens):**

It is correctly tagged direction=POLICY, it reads NO CLEWS signal (it just mutates alpha_RM_1/alpha_RM_T/g_RM/eta_RM from user args), and the feasibility doc is candid that the CLEWS tie is "financial-thematic, not biophysical" (NEW-CHANNELS-FEASIBILITY.md:144, table row "CLEWS signal in PEP? none (financial, via investment channel)"). Net: honestly-scoped as a policy lever, but the economics-of-incidence framing is wrong on both sign and mechanism, and the g_rm path is broken.
- *Coding:* The alpha_RM level-setting arm is largely correct: additive/multiplicative/absolute paths, the max(.,0) floor, persist=True/False (alpha_RM_1 vs alpha_RM_T mapping to OG-Core's get_RM t=1 vs SS-tail), and the no-aliasing copy of eta_RM are all sound, and the docstring's resource-constraint framing (RM subtracted in resource_constraint, added to budgets) matches aggregates.resource_constraint (line 539) and get_RM. ### P0 / P1 findings

**P1 · Welfare-reading guidance is sign-flipped and economically wrong: OG-Core ADDS remittances as a national windfall, it does not subtract them as redistribution** _(lens: Applicability, confidence: high)_

- **Evidence:** channels.py:457-459 (docstring): "OG-Core SUBTRACTS aggregate RM in the resource constraint while adding it to budgets, so this is a redistribution + labor-supply device, NOT a free national windfall -- read welfare as incidence across eta_RM, not added GDP." Repeated verbatim in validate(), channels.py:499-501. But OG-Core: aggregates.py:539 `rc_error = Y - C - I_d - I_g - G - net_capital_flows + RM + foreign_aid` (RM is +, on the resource-INFLOW side with foreign_aid); SS.py:1116-1128 passes RM_vec_ss into resource_constraint as the +RM arg; household budget household.py:371-379 adds `+ rm` with NO offsetting term. RM is treated identically to foreign_aid.
- **Argument:** The claim is wrong on BOTH the sign and the economics. (1) Sign: OG-Core ADDS RM (`+ RM`), it does not subtract it. (2) Economics: because RM enters the resource constraint on the same side as Y and foreign_aid, it is a net external transfer INTO the resident economy — money sent home by overseas workers, whose foregone consumption sits outside the model boundary. Raising alpha_RM loosens the aggregate resource constraint: residents can consume/invest MORE than they produce. That is precisely a national windfall to residents (aggregate consumption and welfare rise, with a labor-supply income effect on top), NOT a within-economy redistribution. eta_RM only governs the INCIDENCE of that inflow across types; it does not make the inflow net-zero. An analyst who follows this guardrail would dismiss a genuine aggregate welfare/consumption gain as "just redistribution, ignore the GDP" and look only at eta_RM dispersion — exactly backwards. This is the channel's one substantive interpretive instruction and it is dishonest about the mechanism.
- **Fix:** Rewrite both the docstring and validate() to: "OG-Core ADDS aggregate RM to the resource constraint (aggregates.py:539, alongside foreign_aid) and to household budgets, so a higher alpha_RM is a net EXTERNAL RESOURCE INFLOW to residents (a transfer-financed windfall, the donor's cost is offshore and outside the model) plus a labor-supply income effect. Read welfare as BOTH the aggregate consumption/utility gain AND its eta_RM incidence across types — not as a zero-sum redistribution." Keep the eta_RM-incidence note as a second-order point, not as the primary framing.

**P0 · eta_RM tilt renormalizes the GLOBAL total, not each period's sum-to-1 -- breaks aggregate RM conservation OG-Core relies on** _(lens: Coding, confidence: high)_

- **Evidence:** channels.py:486-491: `eta = np.array(p.eta_RM, dtype=float); tot = eta.sum(); ... eta = eta * (tot / eta.sum())`. Real eta_RM is 3D (T+S,S,J)=(400,80,7) (parameters.py:256-265 extrapolates to dims=(T+S,S,J); verified Specifications().eta_RM.shape==(400,80,7) with eta_RM[0].sum()==1.0). OG-Core's household.get_rm (household.py:285,295) computes rm = eta_RM[:len_T,:,j]*RM/(lambda*omega) with NO internal renormalization (grep confirms eta_RM appears only in household.py & parameters.py). I proved aggregate distributed RM = RM_t * sum_{s,j}eta_RM[t]: eta summing to 1.0 -> distributed RM=5.0 (target 5), eta summing to 1.1 -> 5.5 (leaks). Probing the channel on a time-varying eta_RM whose every slice sums to 1: per-period sums after the tilt were [0.999,0.990,1.134,0.935,0.985,0.955,1.002] -- NOT 1.0, while the global total stayed 7.0.
- **Argument:** OG-Core treats eta_RM[t] as a per-period probability distribution that MUST sum to 1 so that sum_{s,j}(lambda*omega-weighted) rm = RM_t (the same RM_t aggregates.resource_constraint subtracts at line 539). Preserving only the GLOBAL sum across all 400 periods lets individual periods drift, silently rescaling each period's distributed remittances above/below RM_t and injecting a resource-constraint inconsistency. This directly falsifies the channel's own comment 'renormalizing to the array's ORIGINAL total so aggregate RM is unchanged' and the docstring's 'aggregate RM ... not a free national windfall' -- the windfall (or shortfall) is exactly what leaks per period. It is invisible only because the fixture is 2D/single-period.
- **Fix:** Renormalize per time-slice, not globally. For the real 3D array: `eta = np.array(p.eta_RM, float); w = np.ones(eta.shape[-1]); w[0] = 2.0; eta = eta * w; eta /= eta.sum(axis=(-2,-1), keepdims=True)`. (Guard the zero-sum case per slice.) This keeps each period summing to 1 and is shape-robust to the 2D fixture too (axis=(-2,-1)).

**P1 · Setting g_rm overwrites the extrapolated length-(T+S) g_RM with a short list -> OG-Core get_RM TPI loop IndexErrors** _(lens: Coding, confidence: high)_

- **Evidence:** channels.py:480-481: `if g_rm is not None: p.g_RM = list(np.atleast_1d(np.asarray(g_rm, dtype=float)))`. og_reform is a fully-constructed Specifications deep-copied in framework.py:177 and fed straight to ogcore runner at framework.py:132 with NO re-extrapolation. OG-Core constructs g_RM at length T+S=400 (verified Specifications().g_RM.shape==(400,)). aggregates.get_RM (aggregates.py:264) does `RM[t] = ((1+p.g_RM[t])/...)` for `t in range(1, p.tG1)` (tG1=20) and again for t in range(tG1,tG2-1) -- called from TPI.py:913/920/1365. Reproduced: after `apply(ctx, g_rm=[0.10,0.08,0.05])`, len(p.g_RM)==3 and indexing g_RM[t] for t in range(1,20) raises 'list index out of range'.
- **Argument:** The channel mutates an already-built Specifications, so it must supply g_RM at the length OG-Core already expects (T+S), not the raw user input. A short list crashes every reform that uses the g_rm option during the SS/TPI solve. The option is silently broken because no test passes g_rm and the default fixture g_RM=[0.03] is never indexed beyond [0] in any transform-level test.
- **Fix:** Broadcast g_rm to the existing g_RM length and pad the SS tail, e.g. reuse the codebase's _fit/_align_to_start idiom or `n = len(np.atleast_1d(p.g_RM)); g = np.atleast_1d(np.asarray(g_rm,float)); full = np.full(n, g[-1]); full[:min(len(g),n)] = g[:n]; p.g_RM = list(full)`. Add a test that passes g_rm and asserts len(p.g_RM)==len(baseline g_RM).

**P1 · eta-tilt test is tautological and uses a 2D eta_RM OG-Core can never consume; cannot catch the per-period normalization bug** _(lens: Coding, confidence: high)_

- **Evidence:** tests/test_channels.py:402-407 asserts only `abs(eta1.sum() - eta0.sum()) < 1e-9` (global sum). The code line it checks, channels.py:490 `eta = eta * (tot/eta.sum())`, is constructed to make exactly that quantity invariant -- the assertion can never fail regardless of correctness. The fixture (test line 37) is `eta_RM=np.full((S,J),1/(S*J))` -- 2D. OG-Core's SS path household.get_rm (household.py:282) does `eta_RM[-1,:,j]`, which IndexErrors on a 2D array (reproduced: 'too many indices for array: array is 2-dimensional, but 3 were indexed'). So the test exercises a shape that never reaches a solve and asserts the one invariant the buggy line trivially satisfies.
- **Argument:** A test whose pass condition is the code's own normalization identity proves nothing about behavior; it re-derives the formula. Worse, the uniform single-period 2D fixture hides the P0: with a real 3D time-varying eta_RM the per-period sum drifts, but the global-sum assertion stays satisfied. The test gives false confidence that 'aggregate RM distribution preserved.'
- **Fix:** Use a 3D (T+S,S,J) eta_RM whose slices sum to 1 with time variation, and assert the OG-Core invariant: `np.allclose(eta1.sum(axis=(-2,-1)), 1.0)` AND that mass shifted toward j=0 (`eta1[...,0].sum() > eta0[...,0].sum()`). Also add coverage for the alpha_rm absolute path, alpha_rm_mult, the max(.,0) floor, and persist=False (alpha_RM_T unchanged).

**P1 · Resource-constraint sign is inverted: OG-Core ADDS remittances (a foreign windfall), it does not subtract them — the 'redistribution, not a windfall' framing is economically wrong** _(lens: Economics, confidence: high)_

- **Evidence:** channels.py:457-459 docstring: 'OG-Core SUBTRACTS aggregate RM in the resource constraint while adding it to budgets, so this is a redistribution + labor-supply device, NOT a free national windfall -- read welfare as incidence across eta_RM, not added GDP.' and the same claim in validate() channels.py:499-501. Ground truth ogcore/aggregates.py:539: 'rc_error = Y - C - I_d - I_g - G - net_capital_flows + RM + foreign_aid' (RM enters with a PLUS sign, identically to foreign_aid). Household budget ogcore/household.py:375: rm added with a plus sign in get_cons. I grepped SS.py/TPI.py/fiscal.py/firm.py and found NO offsetting domestic outflow tied to RM.
- **Argument:** In OG-Core remittances are a net inflow of foreign resources: they relax the economy-wide resource constraint (+RM) and add to household budgets (+rm), with no domestic financing counterpart. That is the textbook treatment of remittances as a current-account credit / transfer from abroad — a positive-sum windfall, not an internal reallocation among domestic agents. The channel asserts the exact opposite (subtraction, zero-sum redistribution). A user who follows the instruction to 'read welfare as eta_RM incidence, not added GDP' will systematically understate the true welfare gain of a remittance boom (and the loss of a bust) and will misattribute its mechanism. The eta_RM tilt does redistribute WHO receives a fixed aggregate, so an incidence reading is valid for the concentrate_low_income arm specifically — but it is wrong as a description of varying the alpha_RM level, which genuinely changes aggregate resources.
- **Fix:** Correct the docstring and validate() text: OG-Core ADDS RM in the resource constraint (aggregates.py:539), so raising alpha_RM is a net foreign inflow / windfall that raises aggregate consumption and welfare, analogous to foreign_aid. Distinguish the two arms explicitly: (a) varying alpha_RM_1/_T = a change in the size of the foreign transfer (positive-sum, read as added resources + income-effect labor response); (b) concentrate_low_income with alpha_RM held fixed = pure redistribution of a fixed aggregate (read as eta_RM incidence). Do not tell users to ignore the aggregate welfare/consumption effect.

## Diaspora bonds

**Overall assessments (per lens):**

- *Applicability:* As an OG-only policy lever, diaspora_bonds is honestly labeled (direction=POLICY, theory_status="research", validate() flags it as a reduced-form bond with no biophysical CLEWS signal and discount_bps as a sensitivity axis only). There is genuinely NO reform-differential CLEWS signal feeding it — confirmed: no reader in signals.py touches it, all inputs are caller scalars — and the feasibility doc owns this ("CLEWS link is financial-thematic, not biophysical"). So on the "is the CLEWS<->OG link labeled honestly" question, it passes. The serious problem is mechanism attribution, not framing: the central "patriotic discount" claim — that lowering world_int_rate_annual gives the GOVERNMENT a discounted cost of funds on its diaspora bond — is mechanically false in OG-Core. The government debt-service rate r_gov is a function of the DOMESTIC r only (r_gov_scale*r - r_gov_shift + ...); world_int_rate never enters r_gov, and under PHL's zeta_K=0.9 it does not even pin the domestic r (only zeta_K==1 does that). Lowering world_int_rate is an economy-wide private-foreign-capital-supply shock, not a sovereign financing discount, so the cost-of-funds story is misattributed. Secondarily, with the default foreign_share=None the channel leaves zeta_D untouched, so a default-invoked "diaspora bond" is mechanically indistinguishable from a generic debt-financed alpha_I bump — the foreign-financing identity that makes it a "diaspora bond" is never expressed unless the caller opts in. The matching test locks in the mislabeled behavior without testing the claimed effect.
- *Coding:* The alpha_I leg is correct: route_revenue(to="public_investment", fill_ss_tail=False) bumps alpha_I for the first `years` periods and leaves the SS tail at baseline, which exactly matches OG-Core's consumption (fiscal.py:459-461: I_g = alpha_I[:p.T]*Y for TPI, alpha_I[-1]*Y for SS) under the actual OG-PHL closure (baseline_spending=False, budget_balance=False, so alpha_I is consumed, not alpha_bs_I). The zeta_D leg also works (consumed directly in fiscal.py:193/256). The double-write guard against the `investment` channel is genuine and correct. HOWEVER the "patriotic discount" leg is a SILENT NO-OP in the real pipeline: it mutates p.world_int_rate_annual after the Specifications object is fully built and deep-copied, but OG-Core's SS.py/TPI.py consume the DERIVED p.world_int_rate, which is computed once via rate_conversion() inside update_specifications (parameters.py:283) and is never recomputed by solve()/runner. I proved empirically that mutating world_int_rate_annual leaves world_int_rate unchanged. The channel's own test only asserts on the unconsumed world_int_rate_annual attribute against a SimpleNamespace fixture that has no derived world_int_rate, so it is tautological and fails to catch the no-op. One P1 (the no-op + too-weak test) and minor nits.
- *Economics:* The investment LEG of this channel is mechanically correct: routing issuance to alpha_I feeds I_g = alpha_I*Y -> the K_g law of motion (fiscal.get_I_g/get_K_g), identical to the validated `investment` channel, and the finite window with fill_ss_tail=False correctly leaves the SS tail untouched. As a "recurring %-GDP public-investment program" it is sound. The problem is the FINANCING side, which is the channel's entire claimed contribution ("diaspora-financed", "patriotic discount", "burden is future debt service at the world rate", "discounted cost of funds"). All three financing claims are either no-ops or misattributed under the way OG-Core actually consumes these primitives: (1) the patriotic discount lowers world_int_rate_annual, but world_int_rate enters the equilibrium r ONLY where zeta_K==1, and OG-PHL's default closure has zeta_K=0.9 everywhere (mask selects 0 of 400 periods) — so the discount is a silent no-op that changes nothing. (2) Foreign debt service in OG-Core is r_p*D_f (firm.get_debt_service_f uses the DOMESTIC portfolio return r_p), NOT the world rate — so "burden is future debt service at the world rate" is mechanically false and the "discounted cost of funds" benefit does not exist. (3) Raising zeta_D shifts debt holding abroad but debt service is still r_p*D_f, so it changes only the external-accounting split (net_capital_outflows), and if anything routes MORE interest out of the domestic economy — economically the opposite of a financing benefit. The matching test is tautological: it asserts only that world_int_rate_annual was written lower, never that it has any effect, and runs on a SimpleNamespace lacking zeta_K so the no-op can never surface. The validate() docstring is honest that this is reduced-form, but it materially overstates that the discount and zeta_D create a cheaper foreign financing channel. Net: the investment bump is fine; the financing apparatus that distinguishes "diaspora bonds" from the plain `investment` channel is non-functional or misattributed.

**P1 · "Patriotic discount" misattributes a private-capital-supply shock as a government cost-of-funds discount (OG-Core never routes world_int_rate to debt service)** _(lens: Applicability, confidence: high)_

- **Evidence:** channels.py:533-537 sets p.world_int_rate_annual = wr0 - discount_bps/1e4 and the docstring (channels.py:516-517) claims "the benefit is crowded-in public capital at a discounted cost of funds". But OG-Core's government debt-service rate is fiscal.get_r_gov (fiscal.py:415-432): r_gov = max(r_gov_scale*r - r_gov_shift + r_gov_DY*DY + r_gov_DY2*DY^2, 0). Verified by inspection: 'world_int_rate' in source(get_r_gov) == False. debt_service = r_gov*D (fiscal.py:200) and foreign debt service is also at r_gov on D_f. world_int_rate only pins the domestic r when zeta_K==1 (TPI.py:828 `r[p.zeta_K==1]=p.world_int_rate[...]`; SS.py:490-493), and PHL baseline zeta_K=0.9, so r is set by firm.get_r on the domestic MPK. world_int_rate enters only as the open-economy capital-supply PRICE (SS.py:471-478 get_K(world_int_rate,...) -> K_demand_open; aggregates.py:607 K_f = zeta_K*(K_demand_open - B + D_d)).
- **Argument:** Economically the discount_bps lever does NOT cheapen the sovereign's borrowing. It lowers the world rate at which ALL foreign investors supply capital to the whole private economy, raising K_demand_open -> K_f -> K, lowering the domestic MPK and hence r and r_gov only as a general-equilibrium by-product affecting EVERY firm and the entire debt stock. So the channel attributes an economy-wide private-foreign-capital subsidy to a targeted government diaspora-bond instrument. That is the wrong mechanism for the stated story (a below-market patriotic tranche funding I_g): the discount is applied to the marginal product of the entire capital stock, not to the bond, and its effect on the government's cost of funds is incidental and indirect, not the direct discount the docstring/validate/feasibility doc describe.
- **Fix:** Stop claiming a government cost-of-funds discount via world_int_rate. Either (a) re-label discount_bps honestly as an economy-wide foreign-capital-supply price shock (and note it is NOT specific to the bond and flows to all private capital), or (b) if a sovereign-specific concession is wanted, model it on the government's own rate via r_gov_shift[t] over the issuance window (a wedge on r_gov), not world_int_rate. Update the docstring (channels.py:516-517) and validate() text and the feasibility doc lines 156-159/160-163 accordingly.

## Food price

**Overall assessments (per lens):**

- *Applicability:* food_price is honestly labeled as an OG-only / external-data lever, not a CLEWS<->OG channel: validate() and theory_status="reduced_form" plainly state the live CLEWS emits no crop signal (PHL_LND_CRP=0) and yield_loss must come from IRRI/IFPRI. I verified that claim — the CLEWS only carries agricultural ENERGY demand, no crop production/yield/price — so the "external-driven" framing is correct and the absolute-shock (no base-vs-reform differential) honesty caveat is present. The OG plumbing is also broadly sound: tau_c[food] feeds get_ptilde/get_ci/get_cons under a Cobb-Douglas aggregator (so a food wedge with alpha_c=0.357 has real welfare leverage), c_min[food] is a genuine Stone-Geary regressivity amplifier, and Z[NatRes] does propagate to producer prices via get_pm (p_m = w/MPL) -> p_i = io_matrix @ p_m. The real problem is on the applicability/aggregation lens: the two routes are presented and tested as interchangeable "alternatives" of the same shock, but the food CONSUMPTION good sources only 11.97% from Natural Resources and 67.86% from Manufacturing (food-processing), so the Z[NatRes] route delivers ~6x a smaller and structurally different food-price impulse than the tau_c route, and "Natural Resources" is a 19-sub-sector aggregate (crops + mining + fishing + forestry + water + tobacco) that is not "crops." These are functional/mapping defects, not magnitude nits.
- *Coding:* The tau_c route is coded correctly and matches the energy_price plumbing it clones: it copies (not mutates) p.tau_c, applies the multiplicative wedge (1+price_rise)*(1+tau)-1 across the full T+S length so it correctly reaches the SS tail (verified: tail==head), touches only the food good index (others untouched), and writes a length-I c_min. tax.cons_tax_liab and household.get_ci/get_cons consume tau_c[T+S,I] and c_min[I] exactly as the channel sets them, and Stone-Geary c_min does produce the claimed regressive incidence. However, the Z route has a real structural-mapping bug: it cuts Z[NatRes] (industry 0) to represent a FOOD-good price shock, but OG-Core forms the food-good price as p_i = io_matrix.food . p_m, and the food good draws only 0.1197 of its value from Natural Resources versus 0.6786 from Manufacturing (where food-processing afood/abeve live). So a -10% Z[NatRes] reaches food only at ~12% weight, and under the OG-PHL default epsilon=1 (Cobb-Douglas) Z drops out of p_m entirely (Z^((eps-1)/eps)=Z^0), so the food-price response is GE-mediated and tiny — NOT the mechanical "alternative representation of the same shock" the docstring/validate claim. Secondary coding issues: no input guards (yield_loss>=1 explodes the tau via the 1e-6 floor; yield_loss>1 drives Z negative, breaking the CES production); the food c_min branch lacks the solve-safety guard the sibling energy channel has despite food being the higher-risk case; validate() is a static string that ignores its args/route/active; and both food tests are tautological (they re-derive the apply() formula rather than independently verifying behavior, and never assert the non-target goods/industries are left unchanged).
- *Economics:* The food_price channel gets the high-level economics right — a crop yield loss raises food prices, food is the dominant non-services consumption good (alpha_c=0.357, verified via ogphl.input_output), the Cobb-Douglas demand in household.get_ci falls when tau_c rises, get_ptilde correctly compounds the wedge into the composite price with the 0.357 exponent (so welfare leverage is real), and the Stone-Geary c_min[food] floor is a correct regressivity mechanism (min_cons_expenditure in get_cons subtracts a uniform absolute subsistence cost that bites poor households hardest). The tau_c price-rise functional form (pass_through*yl/(1-yl)) is a defensible reduced form and the sign is correct. BUT two distinct economic problems are present. (1) The Z[NatRes] route is mis-mapped: the OG-PHL io_matrix shows the FOOD consumption good is only 12% sourced from Natural Resources and 68% from Manufacturing food-processing, and NatRes bundles mining (amine) and water (awatr) alongside crops — so Z[NatRes]*=(1-yl) is an economy-wide TFP shock that raises EVERY good's price (Food only ~1.3% at yl=0.10) and haircuts mining/water TFP, not a food-specific crop shock. (2) The two routes are NOT economically equivalent "alternatives" as the docstring/validate claim: the tau_c route books the price rise as domestic consumption-tax revenue (cons_tax_liab -> total_tax_revenue) which the default closure recycles to households, whereas a climate yield loss / import-price pass-through is a real resource loss or a terms-of-trade transfer abroad that creates NO domestic revenue (the Z route correctly produces none). The tau_c route therefore partially refunds the shock and understates the welfare loss, and the channel never flags this — unlike the sibling carbon/energy channels which do.

The two have opposite welfare arithmetic: tau_c[food] costs the economy only a triangle (DWL) and actually hands the government more revenue, whereas a terms-of-trade loss is a first-order income transfer abroad. Using tau_c also mislocates incidence onto domestic food consumers (regressive, all households) when the true incidence is on export-sector producers/workers. The correct OG analog for the lost real income is a negative foreign resource flow (negative alpha_FA, see separate finding) or an export-sector TFP/Z drag, not a domestic consumption distortion.
- **Fix:** Do not use tau_c to represent an export-preference loss. Model the terms-of-trade income loss as a foreign resource outflow via a negative alpha_FA increment (it has the right sign in both the budget and the resource constraint), or as a productivity drag on the export industry's Z[m]. If a consumption-side proxy is unavoidable, document explicitly that it captures only a DWL and NOT the terms-of-trade income transfer, and reconcile the welfare reading accordingly.

**P1 · ODA/aid cut routed through alpha_G (government consumption) instead of OG-Core's dedicated foreign-aid primitive alpha_FA -- cutting alpha_G frees domestic resources (welfare-raising), the opposite economic content of losing aid** _(lens: Economics, confidence: high)_

- **Evidence:** channels.py:801-807 routes the cut to government_consumption: `route_revenue(p, cut, to="government_consumption")`, and policy_levers.py:121-122 maps that to `alpha_G`. But OG-Core's aid primitive is alpha_FA: fiscal.py:98 `foreign_aid = p.alpha_FA[:p.T] * Y[:p.T]`, which (i) relaxes the government budget -- fiscal.py:122 `- foreign_aid[t-1]` reduces debt accumulation, fiscal.py:131 `+ foreign_aid[t]` funds the G closure -- and (ii) is a net foreign INFLOW in the economy's resource constraint: aggregates.py:539 `rc_error = Y - C - I_d - I_g - G - net_capital_flows + RM + foreign_aid` (note the + sign). alpha_G instead is government consumption G = alpha_G*Y, which appears with a - sign in the same RC (it absorbs domestic resources).
- **Argument:** Losing ODA should make the country genuinely poorer: alpha_FA falling removes a foreign resource inflow (RC + term shrinks) and forces the government to borrow more or spend less. Cutting alpha_G does neither -- it just reallocates a fixed domestic pie away from public consumption toward private C/I, which under standard OG welfare arithmetic tends to RAISE household welfare/output (less crowding out by unproductive G). So the channel's lever (c) likely produces a welfare effect with the wrong sign relative to a true aid loss, and it omits the foreign-resource-loss entirely. alpha_FA is in the OG-PHL run path (ogcore default 0.0; settable) and is the only primitive that carries aid's dual budget+RC role.
- **Fix:** Route the aid cut to a negative alpha_FA increment (e.g. add a 'foreign_aid' destination to route_revenue mapping to p.alpha_FA, or set p.alpha_FA directly). This both removes the foreign inflow from the resource constraint and tightens the government budget, matching the economics of an ODA decline. Reserve alpha_G for a deliberate public-spending-cut scenario, which is a different experiment.
